___E-Commerce-Customer-Purchase-Analysis-System___

🚀 Excited to share my latest Data Science & Predictive Analytics Project!

I recently built an end-to-end E-Commerce Customer Purchase Analysis System from scratch. The goal was to transform raw transactional data into actionable business intelligence and deploy predictive models to forecast customer behavior.

Here is a breakdown of the pipeline I engineered using Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn, Seaborn, & Matplotlib):

🔹 Data Preprocessing & Cleaning: Managed inconsistent features, implemented median/mode imputation strategies for missing values, and handled time-series alignment using forward/backward fills.

🔹 Feature Engineering & Simulation: Modeled core purchasing metrics like Total Revenue. To address the absence of explicit customer feedback, I simulated a randomized Rating matrix aligned with transaction distributions.

🔹 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Aggregated metrics at the product level (Product_SKU) to analyze cumulative revenues and pricing structures. Isolated geographic purchase concentrations to optimize inventory insights.

🔹 Data Visualization: Developed high-impact visuals including localized product revenue bar charts, a clean category distribution pie chart with an externalized percentage legend to prevent text overlapping, and demographic city-wise customer countplots.

🔹 Machine Learning: Trained a Multiple Linear Regression model to predict customer total spending based on price architectures, purchase quantities, and ratings (80/20 train-test split), evaluating performance via MAE and $R^2$ Score.

💡 Key Business Insights Extracted:

High-Value Driving Categories: Identified precise product categories driving maximum revenue.

Geographic Sweet Spots: Isolated cities with the largest consumer footprint for targeted marketing.

Revenue Drivers: Quantified how incremental shifts in pricing and quantity choices dictate total customer spending.

This project was a fantastic exercise in balancing rigorous data hygiene with statistical modeling to answer real-world e-commerce problems.
